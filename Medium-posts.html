Criticism, fiction and other writing

Some of my posts on Medium

Towards the end of 2018, I finally did something I’d been thinking about, on and off, for 20 years: I registered an .ie (Irish) domain name. Throughout 2017 and most of 2018, I had been posting most of my writing, fiction and non, on Medium. At first I wasn’t sure how I was going to divide my sparse output between my own site and Medium but it eventually settled into a pattern.

My posts on Medium had tended to fall into one of three general categories. First there was the fiction, mostly short but including one novel-length story and a novella. Then, from April 2018, there was a series of posts on the subject of aphantasia (the lack of a visual imagination, or the inability to form mental images), which I learned in my late 50s that I’ve had all my life). And finally, there was my critical book discussion, in which I took a look in greater depth than a typical review at anything from Nelson DeMille’s mystery The General’s Daughter to the poetry of William Empson and Andrew Marvell, by way of Kazuo Ishiguro’s Never Let Me Go. I’ve treated each of these three categories slightly differently.

I’ve now moved most of the fiction to this site. The exceptions are the novel (which I’m in the course of moving now) and a three-part story I’m not sure about and that I may decide to rewrite substantially. The aphantasia posts, in contrast, remain on Medium where they’re still attracting readers. Instead of moving them, I’ve created a page on this site which lists them all, and to which each of them links back. As for the book discussion, I’ve left most of the posts that were originally on Medium where they are, and put the more recent ones here.

That leaves those posts on Medium that don’t fit into any of these three categories. There are about 16 of these that I think are worth keeping, and I’ve listed them below in reverse chronological order.

  • What Colm Tóibín said about genre fiction — writing
    What did Colm Tóibín actually say about genre fiction — and what did he mean by it? Is it possible that some Irish crime fiction authors may have been too defensive in their responses?
  • What (if anything) is Pinterest good for? — social media
  • Why ebooks aren’t real books — publishing
    The limitations of ebooks — most of them arbitrarily imposed, but some inherent in the nature of the format — make them an inferior reading experience to printed books.
  • Eluding the algorithm — search and discovery
    Algorithmic search is really just a form of recommendation engine. We need better ways of finding things that interest us on the internet.
  • On the keyboard — writing
    If you’re a writer, the most important component (hardware or soft) of any computer you’ll ever use is the keyboard
  • Sophie Hannah plans her novels before she starts to write — writing
    All authors plan their books. Some do it before they write a word, others do it all mixed up in the process of writing. Sophie Hannah is clearly one of the former. But there’s not as big a difference between the two types as you might think.
  • Thanks Twitter, it’s been fun — social media
    I deleted my Twitter account after 9 years. it was easier than I had expected.
  • Novelette: a suitable form for crime fiction? — writing and publishing
    Revolutionary changes in publishing are making shorter forms of fiction more viable. But “shorter forms” doesn’t just mean short stories. We should be starting to see a greater flexibility as to story lengths.
  • Uncollected short stories — writing and publishing
    Publishers and readers have been wary of short stories because economics have dictated that they be published in collections the approximate length of a novel. The changes in publishing may make it easier to present them in a more appealing form.
  • The truth about fiction" — writing
    Getting people to read your fiction online is hard, but that’s OK.
  • Why I’m no longer a self-published author — writing and publishing
    I’ve withdrawn A Falling Body from Apple Books, Kindle and Kobo stores and I’m not going to be returning to those outlets.
  • Goodreads and the self-published author — writing and publishing
    Although Goodreads offers author profiles, it’s really a platform for readers (and not all of those), rather than writers. The two don’t usually mix well, except in the controlled conditions of public readings and conferences.
  • Some of the best fiction writers on Medium — writing and publishing
    The first group of writers whose stories have appeared more than once in my “Recommended short fiction on Medium” newsletter.
  • Publishing long form fiction on Medium — publishing
    If Wattpad has an advantage over Medium for the fiction writer, it’s in the way it makes it easy to post multipart stories. But it can be done just as well on Medium with just a little extra work. This is how.
  • Recommended short fiction on Medium — writing
    The stories recommended in my newsletter, “Recommended short fiction on Medium” during its first six months of existence, from June to December 2017.
  • The language of Ireland
    Attempts to revive the Irish language have been consistently failing for over a century, and will continue to do so. Yet there is no political will to stop wasting time and money on them.
